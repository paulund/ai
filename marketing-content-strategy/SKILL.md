## Core Workflow

1. **Gather context** — ask about the business, target audience, content goals, and what content exists already. Do not generate a strategy without this.
2. **Define content pillars** — identify 3-5 core themes that represent the intersection of what the audience cares about and what the business can speak to with authority.
3. **Classify content** — separate searchable content (captures existing demand) from shareable content (creates demand). A good strategy needs both.
4. **Keyword research by stage** — map content topics to buyer journey stages using proven keyword modifiers.
5. **Prioritise topics** — score ideas against the prioritisation framework and identify the first 8-12 pieces to produce.
6. **Output the strategy** — deliver as a structured document with pillars, prioritised topic list, and 30-day content calendar.

## Content Classification

Every piece of content is one of two types. Most strategies need both.

### Searchable content (captures existing demand)

People are already searching for this. It shows up in Google and AI search results.

- **Use-case content** — "How to use [product] for [specific job]"
- **Hub-and-spoke clusters** — one comprehensive pillar page with supporting articles on related sub-topics
- **Template libraries** — reusable resources people bookmark and share
- **Comparison and alternatives pages** — "[Your product] vs [Competitor]", "Best [category] tools"
- **How-to guides** — step-by-step instructions for solving specific problems

### Shareable content (creates demand)

People do not search for this, but it spreads when it resonates.

- **Original data or research** — surveys, benchmarks, original analysis
- **Thought leadership** — opinions backed by experience, not just assertions
- **Case studies** — real customer stories with specific outcomes and numbers
- **Behind-the-scenes** — how decisions are made, what was tried and failed
- **Contrarian takes** — disagreeing with consensus with evidence and reasoning

## Content Pillars Framework

Content pillars are the 3-5 core themes your content is built around. Good pillars are:

- Specific enough to guide topic selection (not "software development" — too broad)
- Broad enough to sustain months of content (not "a specific bug fix" — too narrow)
- Credible — you can speak about these with real experience and depth
- Aligned — they attract the audience who would buy your product or follow your brand

**How to define pillars:**
1. List the top 5 problems your target audience has
2. List the 5 topics you or your company can speak about with genuine authority
3. Find the overlap — those are your pillars

## Keyword Research by Buyer Stage

Map content to where the buyer is in their journey:

| Stage | What they want | Keyword modifiers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Awareness | Understand a problem or concept | "what is", "how to", "why does", "guide to" |
| Consideration | Compare options, find the best approach | "best", "top", "vs", "comparison", "alternatives" |
| Decision | Evaluate and choose a specific product | "pricing", "reviews", "demo", "free trial" |
| Implementation | Learn to use what they chose | "tutorial", "setup", "templates", "examples" |

A balanced strategy has content at every stage. Most companies over-index on awareness and ignore decision-stage content.

## Prioritisation Framework

Score each topic idea against four factors:

| Factor | Weight | What it measures |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer Impact | 40% | How much does ranking for this topic affect a real customer's decision? |
| Content-Market Fit | 30% | Can you produce genuinely useful content on this topic with real depth? |
| Search Potential | 20% | Is there sufficient search volume? Are you likely to rank? |
| Resource Requirements | 10% | How much effort does this piece take to produce well? |

Score each 1-5 on each factor, apply the weights, rank the list. The top 8-12 items are your starting backlog.

## 30-Day Content Calendar Format

Week 1-2: Foundation
- 2 searchable pillar pieces (high search volume, strong business alignment)
- 1 shareable piece (thought leadership or original data)

Week 3-4: Extension
- 2 supporting articles for pillar 1
- 1 consideration-stage comparison or alternative piece
- 1 shareable piece (case study or behind-the-scenes)

Adjust based on available resources. Consistency beats volume.

## Constraints

### MUST DO

- Ask about business goals and target audience before generating any strategy
- Define content pillars before suggesting topics
- Include both searchable and shareable content in every strategy
- Map topics to specific buyer journey stages
- Output prioritised topic list with rationale, not just a brainstorm
- Include a concrete 30-day starting calendar

### MUST NOT DO

- Generate topics without knowing what the business does and who it serves
- Produce a generic strategy that could apply to any company
- Recommend only awareness-stage content — every strategy needs decision-stage content too
- Output hundreds of topic ideas — focus on 8-12 high-priority starting points
- Confuse content pillars with content categories — pillars are themes, not buckets
