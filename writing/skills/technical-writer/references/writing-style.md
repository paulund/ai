# Writing Style Reference

This reference guide provides best practices for writing style in technical documentation. It covers tone, voice, clarity, conciseness, and organisation to ensure that technical content is accessible and effective for its intended audience.

## Tone

- **Direct and blunt** — say what you mean, cut qualifications that soften the point. If something is a bad idea, say so.
- **First-person** — write from personal experience. "I've seen this break in production" beats "this can sometimes cause issues".
- **Informal** — write like you're explaining to a colleague, not writing for a company blog. No corporate tone, no hedging for the sake of sounding balanced.

## Best Practices

- **Tone and voice**: Use a direct, informal tone. Write in the active voice to make sentences clearer and more direct.
- **Clarity**: Use simple and straightforward language. Avoid jargon and technical terms unless necessary, and provide explanations when they are used.
- **Conciseness**: Be concise and to the point. Eliminate unnecessary words and redundant information.
- **Organisation**: Structure content logically with clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points. Use numbered lists for step-by-step instructions.
- **Examples and visuals**: Include examples, code snippets, and visuals (e.g., diagrams, screenshots) to enhance understanding and illustrate concepts.
- **Consistency**: Maintain consistency in terminology, formatting, and style throughout the documentation.
- **Audience awareness**: Tailor the content to the knowledge level and needs of the intended audience. Avoid assumptions about prior knowledge.
- **Accuracy**: Verify all technical information with subject matter experts to ensure accuracy and reliability.
- **Revision and editing**: Review and edit the documentation for clarity, grammar, and spelling. Consider peer reviews for additional feedback.
- **Accessibility**: Ensure that the documentation is accessible to all users, including those with disabilities. Use alt text for images and follow accessibility guidelines.
