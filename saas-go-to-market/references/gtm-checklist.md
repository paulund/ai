# Go-To-Market Checklist

## Positioning Worksheet

Complete this before any channel or content decisions.

**The core question:** What does this do, for whom, and why is it better than what they use today?

### Fill in the blanks

**Target customer:**
- Job title or role:
- Company size (if B2B):
- Industry (if specific):
- What they are trying to accomplish:

**The problem:**
Write one sentence describing the specific pain the product addresses. No jargon. If a customer described this problem to a colleague, what would they say?

**Current alternatives:**
What does the customer use today instead of your product? (Competitor / spreadsheet / manual process / nothing)

**Value proposition:**
What specific outcome does your product deliver? (Use a number if possible: "saves 4 hours per week", "reduces churn by 20%", "cuts deploy time from 2 hours to 15 minutes")

**Key differentiator:**
One thing that makes this product better or different from the current alternative. Be honest — not a list of features. The one most compelling reason to switch.

**Positioning statement:**
> For [target customer] who [have this problem], [product name] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [current alternative], [product name] [key differentiator].

## Launch Phase Checklist

### Phase 1-2: Internal and Alpha (done before beta)

- [ ] Core product works end-to-end
- [ ] Must-have features from launch checklist are complete
- [ ] 3-5 alpha users identified (existing relationships, not random)
- [ ] Feedback collection process in place

### Phase 3: Beta (before early access)

- [ ] 20-50 beta users from target audience (not just friends)
- [ ] Clear success metric defined for beta (activation rate, retention, NPS)
- [ ] At least 2-3 users who would give a genuine testimonial
- [ ] Known bugs and UX issues addressed
- [ ] Pricing decided (even if not yet charging)

### Phase 4: Early Access (before full launch)

- [ ] Landing page with email capture live
- [ ] Waitlist email sequence set up
- [ ] 2-3 case studies or testimonials with specific outcomes
- [ ] Demo video or interactive demo available
- [ ] Legal pages published (ToS, Privacy Policy)
- [ ] Support channel operational
- [ ] Analytics configured
- [ ] 50-200 people on waitlist from target audience

### Phase 5: Full Launch

**1 week before:**
- [ ] All launch assets ready (screenshots, demo, testimonials, press kit)
- [ ] Social posts drafted and scheduled
- [ ] Email to waitlist drafted and tested
- [ ] Borrowed channel partners briefed and confirmed
- [ ] Technical load testing done (if expecting significant traffic)
- [ ] On-call plan: who monitors and responds to issues on launch day?

**Launch day:**
- [ ] Email waitlist (send at optimal time for audience timezone)
- [ ] Post on all rented channels simultaneously
- [ ] Submit to Product Hunt (if a fit — requires genuine community engagement, not just posting)
- [ ] Activate all borrowed channel partnerships
- [ ] Respond to every comment, reply, and question within 2 hours
- [ ] Monitor error tracking and performance dashboards

**Post-launch (week 1):**
- [ ] Track signups by source — which channels performed?
- [ ] Talk to every user who signed up but did not activate
- [ ] Send follow-up to everyone who opened but did not sign up
- [ ] Write a launch retrospective (what worked, what did not)

## Channel-Specific Tactics

### X (Twitter)

- Post consistently (3-5x per week) before launch to build an audience
- Engage in conversations in your niche — replies to relevant accounts
- Build-in-public posts during development generate early interest
- Thread format for product announcements
- Do not rely on launch-day virality — build the audience before you need it

### LinkedIn

- Focus on the professional problem your product solves
- Story-led posts about the journey, decisions, and lessons
- Engage with your target audience's content before your launch
- Company page less important than personal brand for early-stage

### Reddit and Hacker News

- Be a genuine community member before promoting anything
- Show HN post on Hacker News works when: the project is technically interesting, the post is honest and self-deprecating, and you respond to every comment
- Reddit: find subreddits where your audience gathers, contribute value before sharing your product, read community rules carefully

### Email / newsletter

- Start building the list before you have a product
- Offer something immediately useful in exchange for the email (guide, template, early access)
- Weekly or fortnightly newsletter keeps the list warm
- Launch email subject: "We're live" is less effective than "The thing you signed up for is ready"

### Product Hunt

- Best for: B2B SaaS and developer tools
- Requires a hunter with reach, or building your own follower base first
- Prepare: a compelling tagline (60 characters), gallery of screenshots, demo GIF, and a compelling first comment
- Launch on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday for best traffic
- Mobilise your email list and community to upvote on launch day
- Respond to every comment on launch day
- Do not treat Product Hunt as a strategy — treat it as one distribution channel with a specific audience

## Positioning Mistakes to Avoid

- **Category confusion** — if you cannot say what category you are in, customers cannot either
- **Feature-led messaging** — "We have [feature]" instead of "You get [outcome]"
- **Everybody** — "for all businesses" means you are for no one. Narrow the audience.
- **Too many differentiators** — if you claim ten things that make you different, none of them land
- **Vague comparisons** — "better than the alternatives" without naming them and being specific
- **Premature positioning** — positioning before talking to 20+ customers is guessing, not strategy
